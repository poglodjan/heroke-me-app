FX Forward
The simplest way to hedge your amount
Collar
Protects against unfavorable moves while allowing limited upside
Participator
A derivative allowing partial participation in favorable currency movements while providing protection against unfavorable ones.
Options
Provides the right, but not the obligation, to trade at a set rate.
Options with rebate
Receives a rebate if the option is not exercised.
Spreads
Limits the cost of options by capping potential gains.
Capped Forward
A forward contract with a maximum limit on the value that can be reached during the contract's duration.
Loss Capped Forward
A forward contract that limits the maximum loss, protecting against large losses while still allowing for gains.
Leveraged Forward
A forward contract where gains or losses are strengthened, allowing for a higher potential return, but also increased risk.
Step Down Forward
A forward contract where the rate decreases at predefined trigger, reducing the potential gains and limiting losses.
Step Down Collar
A Collar contract where the rate decreases at predefined trigger, reducing the potential gains and limiting losses.
Convertible Forward
Provides downside protection while limiting potential gains by locking the exchange rate at a predefined trigger, allowing partial participation in favorable market movements.
Convertible Collar
Provides downside protection while limiting potential gains by locking the exchange rate at a predefined (better than forward) trigger, allowing partial participation in favorable market movements.
Knock-Out Forward
A forward contract that automatically terminates if the exchange rate reaches a predetermined trigger during the contract period.
Knock-Out Forward with Subsidy
A knock-out forward contract where the cost is partially subsidized, offering more favorable terms under certain conditions.
Knock-Out Collar
A collar option that expires when the exchange rate hits a specific threshold, providing protection but limiting participation.
Knock-Out Collar with Subsidy
A knock-out collar with a subsidy that reduces the cost of the contract, expiring if a certain exchange rate is reached.
TARN/TARF
A derivative where the buyer receives enhanced returns for favorable currency movements, but the contract ends once a CAP payout is met.
